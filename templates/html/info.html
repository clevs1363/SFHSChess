About Our Club

How to Join

It actually really easy to join SFHS Chess Club! We meet every Friday, or Thursday if there's no school. For special added bonuses, you can sign in (or sign up) to the website here.

What We Do

Chess Club is a lot of fun to be a part of. Even if you don't play chess, or don't think you're very good at it, you can still enjoy Chess Club! It gives a sense of community, as well as giving you the opportunity to make new friends, and learn a whole new game. In addition to this, we attend tournaments every few months and compete against other schools. Playoffs at the end of the year are dependant on our school's ranking, and prizes are awarded to the top school.


Challenging People

  • You can challenge anyone you like within 5 ranks of yourself. For example, if you are rank 12, you can challenge anyone up to rank 7.
  • You can only challenge those in the top 5 if you are within 3 ranks of them. That is, rank 10 cannot challenge 5, but 8 can.
  • Technically, anyone can challenge anyone of any rank, but if the parameters above are not met, the person who was challenged can decline. (Rank 12 can challenge rank 1, but rank 1 can decline.)
  • After you challenge someone, the member you challenged has a week to accept. If the person has a valid reason for not accepting, a two-week extension is granted. (The captain will determine the validity of an excuse.)
  • If you have just joined the club, and you are the last rank, your first challenge can be anyone outside of the top 5!
  • If you challenge someone and win, and the person you beat challenges you within a week, you have TWO weeks to accept.

Rankings

  • If you challenge someone, and you beat them, you trade ranks.
  • When you first join the club, your rank is initially the lowest in the club. Prepare to battle your way up!
  • On tournament day, the highest-ranked player attending takes 1st board, second highest-ranked takes 2nd board, and so on.

Challenge Matches

  • Unless both players agree, the game is touch move by default.
  • No outside help in any form is permitted. If you are caught doing so, you recieve an automatic loss and a penalty of 3 ranks.
  • If one player cannot finish the game before he has to leave, an outside source takes a picture of the game, and the game is finished the following week.
  • If a player cannot resume the game the following week, the rule of acceptance (under "Challenging People section) applies.

General

  • ALL tournaments are touch move, regardless of what each player agrees to.
  • Each match must be played with a clock.
  • You must touch the clock with the same hand that moved the piece- no exceptions.
  • If a player runs out of time, that player automatically loses the game.
